Good to Know

Honest
answers.

We'd rather you ask now than be surprised later. Here's everything worth knowing before you book.

  • What are the check-in and check-out times?
    Check-in is after 3:00 PM, check-out by 11:00 AM. Self check-in via keypad — no need to coordinate schedules. During peak season, Saturday-to-Saturday stays are standard.
  • The listing shows one bathroom — is that really okay for 8 guests?
    We hear you — and it's a fair question. The honest answer: yes, and here's why. The home has a thoughtful layout, with bedrooms spread across the house, and our guests consistently report it works beautifully. With three separate bedroom wings and a well-designed space, most groups settle into a natural rhythm. It has never been cited in a review as an issue. That said, if your group needs two bathrooms, we want you to know upfront — and it may be worth considering as you plan.
  • Are pets really welcome?
    Yes, genuinely — not just technically. Dogs are welcome at The Tara. The yard, the riverfront access, and the neighborhood are great for dogs. Just let us know when you book.
  • Can we bring our boat or kayak?
    Absolutely. The dock accommodates boats and we have kayaks, canoes, and paddleboards available for your use. The Intermediate River connects to Lake Bellaire and the Southern Chain of Lakes, including Torch Lake.
  • What's the cancellation policy?
    Cancellation terms vary by platform. For peak season direct bookings, we use a firm policy — we'll outline terms clearly when you inquire. We're humans, not algorithms, and we work with guests when real life happens.
  • How far is Torch Lake?
    About 10 minutes by car to public access. But you're also on the Intermediate River right at the house, which is connected to the chain — some guests take the kayaks all the way to Torch Lake directly from the dock.
  • Is the home accessible by someone with limited mobility?
    The main living areas and one bedroom are accessible from the main level. The riverfront dock does involve steps down from the property. We're happy to walk you through specifics — just ask.
